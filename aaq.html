I would be glad to help you with programming your keypad! Please follow the steps below:

CLEARING THE KEYPAD:

1. Press and hold the program and the up/down arrows at the same time; the lights will either flash then go out, or flash then go solid, release.

AT KEYPAD:

1. Press & hold the program button and the up/down button together until the red light stops flashing.

2. Enter 3 -> 5 -> 7, then press and release the program button once.

3. Enter programmed PIN #, then press and release the program button twice.

4. Press and release the number key for the number of units you’ll be programming to keypad (1,2, or 3).

5. Press and release the program button once.

LEARN CODE UNIT: Press and release the learn code button once; the LED indicator will flash red.

PRG/SET UNIT: Press and hold the square program/set button until a solid blue LED appears, release; the LED will flash purple. If you still have a solid blue LED, press and release the program/set button to get the purple flashing LED.

AT KEYPAD:

1. Enter programmed PIN #, then press and release the up/down arrow button once.

2. Press and release the ‘1’ key once.

3. Firmly press and release up/down arrow button until the door operates, then wait until the keypad backlights go out before continuing to the next step.

AT SECOND UNIT:

1. Put the unit into programming mode (see ‘LEARN CODE UNIT’ or ‘PRG/SET UNIT’ instructions above).

AT KEYPAD:

1. Enter programmed PIN #, then press and release the up/down arrow button once.

2. Press and release the ‘2’ key once.

3. Firmly press and release up/down arrow button until the door operates, then wait until the keypad backlights go out before continuing to the next step.

AT THIRD UNIT:

1. Put the unit into programming mode (see ‘LEARN CODE UNIT’ or ‘PRG/SET UNIT’ instructions above).

AT KEYPAD:

1. Enter programmed PIN #, then press and release the up/down arrow button once.

2. Press and release the ‘3’ key once.

3. Firmly press and release up/down arrow button until the door operates, then wait until the keypad backlights go out before continuing to the next step.

Once the backlight on the keypad goes out, you can test the keypad:

1. Enter your programmed PIN #

2. Press and release the up/down arrow button once.

3. Press and release the key that corresponds to the door number (1, 2, or 3); the door should operate.

If you run into any issues during this process, you can start over by going back to the very first step! If your keypad is still having trouble after trying these steps a few times, you can call us at (800) 354-3643, Monday-Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM EST. You can also respond to this email with any questions you have!

I would like to try resetting your limits. Your door will have a ‘down/close limit’ and an ‘up/open limit’. These limits tell the door where to stop when its opening or closing, which makes sure the door doesn’t run into the ground or the powerhead on your ceiling. If these limits aren’t working properly, they can cause damage to your door and opener, or problematic movement in either direction! Please follow the steps below:

You will want to start with your door in the open or partially open position and engaged to the opener (make sure the red cord has not been pulled; if so, engage the carriage back into the bullet/turnbuckle on the chain or belt before proceeding).

DOWN LIMIT:

1. On the belly of the powerhead on the ceiling, you should see two triangles and one square button. Press and hold the down triangle button (-) until the long LED comes on blue; release the button, and the LED will begin to flash blue.

2. Press and hold the down triangle button (-) to close the garage door. When the door lightly touches the ground, release the button. To adjust the position, you can press either the up triangle (+) or the down triangle (-).

3. When the door is at the correct position, press and release the square button once to set the position. Both LEDs should flash blue then go out.

UP LIMIT:

1. Press and hold the up triangle button (+) until the long LED comes on blue; release the button, and the LED will begin to flash blue.

2. Press and hold the up triangle (+) to open the garage door. When the door is all the way open (do not allow it to hit the powerhead), release. To adjust the position, you can press either the up triangle (+) or the down triangle (-).

3. When the door is at the correct position, press and release the square button once to set the position. Both LEDs should flash blue then go out.

Please do not skip this step: After you reset the limits, you’ll have to set the force of your door. This is a necessary step to finish this process.

1. Go to the wall console: press and release the button on the wall to fully close the garage door.

2. Once the door is fully closed, press and release the button on the wall again to open the door all the way.

Your limits should be set properly now! If you continue to have issues, you can try these steps again or you can call us at (800) 354-3643, Monday-Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM EST. You can also respond to this email with any questions you have!

Thank you, and good luck!

Good morning,

The red safety beam blinking 3 times usually indicates either interference from another set of beams or sunlight interference. Do you have sunlight shining on the green beam? If so, this is causing the issue. You can determine this for sure by blocking sunlight from the green beam and seeing if the red beam goes solid.

If the sun is hitting the safety beams, usually the easiest way to correct this is to the swap the positions of the safety beams. Move the red one to where the green one is, and vice versa. The wires can stay the same. If the sun is hitting both sides, then you can move the safety beams back farther into the garage, but they will need to stay at the same height. If this does not solve the issue, or if you’re unable to switch the positions, you can cover the green beam with a small paper towel or toilet paper roll. Make sure not to block the sensor from the red beam.

Please let me know how the red LED responds to these steps. You can respond to this email or call us at (800) 354-3643 any weekday from 8:00 A.M. to 5:00 P.M. EST.
